DIG DEEPER

BY: DAN RAVEN, JOHN ERNEST, ARMAN CARLO

Good Ol' Days

City of Malolos has been a stress-free community before 1970's. The population was not as crowded as it is today. We can see children playing in the streets, vendors selling in the sidewalks that not causing so much blockage. Air quality was on its peak making Maloleños comfortable even in outside their homes. There are many places that are eye catching from our good old days.

Urbanization

Time flies so swiftly that Maloleños didn’t see it coming. Various establishments were built focusing on the commerce and business sector raising the overall income of Malolos. As the opportunities arise, many people around Central Luzon noticed the rapid growth of the humble town. That’s when the so-called town became a specialized city; City Of Malolos. The peaceful town before was devoured by urbanization. 

Obstructed Progress

Commercial and recreational opportunities comes to the city every now and then; malls and subdivisions are common sights around the vicinity. Thus, there is a rapid growth in the quantity of public utility vehicles (PUV's) and private vehicles. This uncontrollable growth induces traffic jam developing locale, especially on 'the busiest road throughout Malolos' - Paseo Del Congreso.
